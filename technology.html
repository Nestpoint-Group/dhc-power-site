Lithium, meet niobium.

DHC Power pairs niobium-based anode chemistry with lithium cells. The result is a battery that charges fast, runs across extreme temperatures, and stays safe — exactly where conventional lithium-ion struggles.

§ 01  /  PRINCIPLE

Why niobium.

Conventional graphite anodes plate lithium when pushed hard or run cold — the root cause of slow charging, degradation, and thermal risk. Niobium-oxide anodes intercalate lithium differently.

FIG 1 / CHARGE — Li⁺ INTERCALATION SCHEMATIC · NOT TO SCALE

An anode that doesn't fight physics.

Niobium-oxide structures host lithium ions across a stable voltage window — high enough to prevent metallic lithium from ever plating on the anode. That single property delivers rapid recharge, reliable cold-weather operation, and a dramatically safer failure mode.

The elevated operating voltage also unlocks aluminum current collectors in place of the heavier, costlier copper used in conventional lithium-ion. Within niobium oxide's operating window, aluminum forms a stable passivation layer that outperforms copper in both electrolyte and ambient environments — delivering lower cost, higher gravimetric energy density, and longer service life across cell and pack assemblies.
